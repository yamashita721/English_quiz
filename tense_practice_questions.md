# English Tense Practice Questions
## High-Quality Speaking & Writing Prompts

---

## 1. Usual Activities (Habit/Routine) – Simple Present
**Level: B1-B2**

1. How do you typically structure your mornings on weekdays, and what part of the routine sets the tone for your day?
2. What habits help you stay productive when you don't feel motivated?
3. How do you balance exercise, work, and downtime during a normal week?
4. What small daily choices do you make that have a big impact over time?
5. How do you usually handle unexpected interruptions during your workday?
6. Which hobbies do you regularly pursue, and why do they stick?

**Key Grammar:** Simple Present (do/does), frequency adverbs (usually, typically, regularly)

---

## 2. Factual Descriptions (Timeless Facts) – Simple Present
**Level: B2-C1**

1. How does your city's public transport system operate during peak hours?
2. What steps does your organization follow to approve a new project?
3. How does photosynthesis convert light energy into chemical energy?
4. What distinguishes a legal precedent from a statute in your jurisdiction?
5. How does your device's backup system store and retrieve data?
6. What features define a high‑context culture compared with a low‑context one?

**Key Grammar:** Simple Present for facts, passive voice, technical/academic vocabulary

---

## 3. Opinions (Beliefs & Evaluations) – Simple Present
**Level: B1-C1**

1. What makes a presentation persuasive, in your experience?
2. Which is more important for career growth: specialization or versatility? Why?
3. Do deadlines improve creativity or stifle it? Explain your reasoning.
4. What criteria do you use to judge whether news is reliable?
5. Which skill should schools prioritize more, and why: coding, media literacy, or financial literacy?
6. Is remote work better for most teams? Defend your stance with examples.

**Key Grammar:** Opinion markers (I think, in my view), comparative structures, modal verbs

---

## 4. Possessions (Have/Own/Belong) – Simple Present
**Level: A2-B1**

1. What tools or resources do you currently have that most improve your work?
2. Which possessions do you value for their utility rather than sentimental reasons?
3. What subscriptions do you maintain, and which give the best value?
4. Which books do you own that you often recommend to others, and why?
5. What equipment do you have that you underuse, and what would help you use it more?
6. What apps belong on your "must‑install" list for a new device?

**Key Grammar:** Have/has, possessive forms, relative clauses (that/which)

---

## 5. Ability Descriptions (Can/Be Able To) – Present Ability
**Level: B1-B2**

1. What tasks can you complete under pressure that you struggle with in calm settings?
2. Which complex topics can you explain clearly to a beginner?
3. In which situations can you multitask effectively, and when can't you?
4. What creative skills can you bring to a team that others might overlook?
5. What constraints limit what you can accomplish in a typical day?
6. How quickly can you learn a new tool, and what accelerates that process?

**Key Grammar:** Can/can't, be able to, modal verbs for ability

---

## 6. Current Status of Happenings – Present Continuous
**Level: B1-B2**

1. What projects are you currently working on, and what's progressing well right now?
2. Which habits are you trying to build at the moment, and how are you tracking them?
3. What challenges are you facing this week that you didn't anticipate?
4. How is your team handling feedback from the latest review cycle?
5. What trends are you noticing in your industry this quarter?
6. Which tasks are you postponing today, and why?

**Key Grammar:** Present Continuous (am/is/are + -ing), time markers (currently, at the moment, right now)

---

## 7. Commands (Imperatives) – Directives & Instructions
**Level: B1-B2**

1. Outline the steps for completing the task, then execute the first two steps.
2. Describe your goal in one sentence. Now prioritize three actions—do them in order.
3. Identify your biggest bottleneck. Remove one obstacle within the next hour.
4. Summarize the meeting in three bullet points. Share the key decision first.
5. Write a concise email request (under 100 words). Send it before noon.
6. Set a 25‑minute timer. Focus on one task. Don't switch contexts.

**Key Grammar:** Imperative form (base verb), sequencing words (first, then, now)

---

## 8. Comments (Descriptions & Feedback) – Present Simple/Continuous
**Level: B2-C1**

1. Your introduction is clear, but the main claim needs evidence—what source can you add?
2. The design looks consistent; however, the call‑to‑action isn't prominent. How can you highlight it?
3. I'm noticing repeated terms—where can you replace them with precise alternatives?
4. The pace is improving, but the transitions still feel abrupt. Where can you add signposting?
5. This solution is elegant; still, how does it handle edge cases?
6. Your examples are relatable; can you include one counterexample for balance?

**Key Grammar:** Present Simple for observations, conjunctions (but, however), question formation

---

## 9. Suggestions (Modals & Conditionals) – Polite Advice
**Level: B2-C1**

1. You could break the project into milestones—what would the first milestone include?
2. You might test the idea with a small pilot. What metrics would validate it?
3. It may help to define "done" for each task. What's your definition for this one?
4. You should consider a simpler baseline model first—what baseline makes sense here?
5. Could you draft two versions and A/B test them? What's your success criterion?
6. If time is limited, which part should you cut without hurting quality?

**Key Grammar:** Modal verbs (should, could, might, may), conditional structures (if + present)

---

## 10. Mixed-Tense Challenge Questions
**Level: B2-C1**

1. What do you usually do to prepare for presentations, and what are you working on improving right now?
2. What resources do you have that can help, and what skills are you currently developing to use them better?
3. What do you believe makes a team effective, and how are you testing that belief this month?
4. What can you already do well, and what will you be able to do after completing your next course?
5. What steps does your review process follow, and which step are you currently revising?

**Key Grammar:** Multiple tenses in one sentence, contrast between habitual and ongoing actions

---

## Answer Key Notes

**For Teachers/Self-Study:**
- No single "correct" answer exists for these open-ended questions
- Evaluate based on:
  - Correct tense usage
  - Grammatical accuracy
  - Appropriate vocabulary
  - Coherent reasoning
  - Natural fluency

**Suggested Use:**
- Speaking practice: 2-3 minutes per question
- Writing practice: 50-100 words per question
- Pair work: Ask and answer, then switch
- Self-recording: Compare recordings over time
